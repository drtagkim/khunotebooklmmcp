### Overview
This report contrasts the traditional "Survey-based" Hallyu Index with the proposed "Hybrid Data-driven" Index. The goal is to highlight the structural innovations that allow the new index to overcome the "lag" and "bias" of current methods.

### Existing Methodology (The Status Quo)
*   **Method**: Annual consumer surveys (N=~20k) and trade statistics.
*   **Strengths**: Established longitudinal data, direct consumer feedback.
*   **Weaknesses**:
    *   *Time Lag*: Data is 6-12 months old at publication.
    *   *Subjectivity*: Survey responses rely on recall and self-reporting.
    *   *Limited Scope*: Often focuses heavily on Drama/K-Pop, missing niche growth.

### Proposed Methodology (The Innovation)
*   **Method**: Real-time Big Data (Search/Social) + Weighted Survey + Log Data.
*   **Innovations**:
    *   *Dynamic "Nowcast"*: Weekly/Monthly updates using search trends.
    *   *Cultural Discount Weighting*: Adjusting scores based on cultural distance (e.g., high popularity in a culturally distant region gets a higher innovation score).
    *   *Lifecycle Tracking*: Tracking content from "Buzz" phase to "Monetization" phase.
