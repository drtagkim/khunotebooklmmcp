## 1. üìÑ Report: Theoretical Framework for Creativity in Cultural R&D

### Executive Summary
This report explores the theoretical methodologies for measuring creativity and innovation specifically within the Cultural R&D sector. Moving beyond traditional output-based metrics (e.g., patent counts), we analyze the "4P Model" of Creativity (Person, Process, Product, Press) and its applicability to K-Culture.

### Key Theoretical Approaches
1.  **Person (Human Capital)**: Measuring the creative competencies of creators and producers.
    *   *Metrics*: Diversity of creative teams, cross-disciplinary skills.
2.  **Process (Innovation Flow)**: Evaluating how ideas are generated, selected, and developed.
    *   *Metrics*: Collaborative networks, risk-taking culture, open innovation adoption.
3.  **Product (Output Quality)**: Assessing the novelty and appropriateness of the final content.
    *   *Metrics*: "Consensual Assessment Technique" (Expert review), stylistic innovation.
4.  **Press (Environment)**: The ecosystem supporting creativity.
    *   *Metrics*: R&D funding stability, freedom of expression, technological infrastructure.

### Conclusion
A robust Hallyu Index must incorporate these theoretical dimensions to capture the *source* of competitiveness, not just the sales figures.
